Kansas man injured during attempt to shoot a buffalo 
RENO COUNTY — Law enforcement authorities and emergency crews in Reno County worked an accidental shooting . NEWLINE Just before 12:30pm Friday Deputies with the Reno County Sheriff ’s Office and Haven EMS were dispatched to Yoder Meats in Yoder for an accidental gun shot victim , according to a social media report . NEWLINE When they arrived they found Duane Helms , Moundridge with a gunshot wound to his right calf . NEWLINE Helms was assisting Justin Roddy , Hutchinson , in trying to shoot a Buffalo so it could be butchered . NEWLINE Roddy , who is employed by Yoder Meats , was on top of a ladder trying to shoot the buffalo that was confined inside of a trailer . NEWLINE The Buffalo kept turning away so Helms walked up to tail end of the trailer to try to get the Buffalo to turn . NEWLINE When the Buffalo did turn he hit the side of the trailer , which jarred the ladder , knocking Roddy off balance and causing the rifle to discharge . NEWLINE The shot struck Helms in the leg . By law you are only allowed to shoot an animal one time , anymore is classified as cruelty to an animal . Weapon was a single shot 223 , according to the Sheriff ’s Department . NEWLINE No additional details were released .
