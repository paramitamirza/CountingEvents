Police confirm child shot in Enid shooting
An 11-year - old boy suffered gunshot wounds in Enid Monday afternoon , city police said .

The child was taken to a hospital with gunshot wounds to his finger and shoulder .
His injuries are non - life - threatening , police said .

A police spokesperson said the shooting happened just about 3:15 p.m. in the 300 block of South Seventh Street .

Witnesses told police a man possibly wearing a red hat , red T - shirt and blue jeans or shorts exited a dark blue or grey car and opened fire on South Seventh toward West Street .

Officers are looking for the gunman .

No other information has been released .
