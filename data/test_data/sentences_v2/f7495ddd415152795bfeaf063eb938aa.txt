Police arrest man in 2-year - old child shooting in north Raleigh
A north Raleigh child was rushed to the hospital after Raleigh Police say he shot himself in the face Friday evening .
It happened at the Falls Creek townhomes in the 5200 block of Evergreen Forest Way .
The area is north of I-540 and east of U.S. 1.The child was rushed to WakeMed .
Officials said the bullet hit him in the chin and he was conscious when he left in the ambulance .
The child was transferred to Duke Hospital on Friday night .
Police arrested 34-year - old Jamick Dakota Corely in connection with the shooting .
He was charged with possession of a firearm by a convicted felon , possession of a firearm with an altered or obliterated serial number , failure to store a firearm safely to protect a minor , and contributing to the delinquency of a minor .
Corely was taken to Wake County Detention Center .
It is not yet clear what Corely 's relationship was with the injured child .
Residents tell ABC11 they did n't hear the shots fired , but saw the chaos after .
Folks rushed out of their homes as sirens blared in the ordinarily quiet neighborhood .
Eyewitness Danny Royale saw EMS frantically placing the toddler in an ambulance."We wanted to see which apartment they were going to , so in like 30 seconds they ran back with the child , put in the ambulance and took off .
The child only wearing pampers , " said Royale .
ABC11 was there as officers retrieved the weapon .
Officers were also seen interviewing people in the back of police cruisers .
Residents say the people who live in the house where the shooting happened moved in a couple months ago .
