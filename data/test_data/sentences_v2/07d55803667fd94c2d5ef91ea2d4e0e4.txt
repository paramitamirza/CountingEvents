10-year - old girl wounded in apparent drive - by shooting
A 10-year - old girl is recovering from a gunshot wound , after being hit while sleeping on a couch at home .

Family members say she will be fine , but the little girl is another shooting victim in a spree of violence across east Columbus .

Some neighbors tell 10TV that living along East 13th Avenue , gunshots are heard often .
One neighbor said she heard them around 1 a.m. , and she didn’t want to look out the window .

Gladys Garland has been checking her mail on the front porch of her 13th Avenue home for nine years .
She raised her children in the home across the street .

" Everybody was close knit .
Everybody knew everybody .
It was a joy because we did n't have to worry , ”
Garland said .

Over the years her neighbors have changed , sights look different , but sounds have become more familiar to her .

"
Quite a bit .
Especially on the weekend,’ Garland said .

Garland heard the gunshots early Wednesday morning come from across the street .

The shooting sent a 10 year old to the hospital .
Family members of the young girl say they were at home when they heard the shots fired .

The girl ’s aunt says the child was sleeping on the couch and didn’t realize she had been shot .
Her aunt tells 10TV over the phone the bullet hit her back and went through her .

East 13th Avenue is now the fifth location that ’s been wrapped in crime tape .
Five shootings , four days three people dead and more are injured .

" I think that there has to be a change for the better , quick because we should not have to live where we are worried and concerned about our next door neighbors , ” Garland said .

Garland says she ’s thinking of moving , but she doesn’t to .
She says she loves her neighborhood , despite the change .

" I just pray .
Ask God to watch over us and protect us , ” she said .

Investigators have not released any suspect information for the East 13th Avenue shooting .

Columbus police have not said if these shootings are gang related or connected at all .
