Fatal Bourbon Street gunfight stemmed from ' feud over woman '
NEW ORLEANS -- New details have emerged about a shooting on Bourbon Street last month that left one man dead and nine others hurt .

 Prosecutors say the shooting stemmed from an argument over a woman .

One of the suspects , Jordan Clay , told detectives that he shot at Travon Manuel in self - defense after the two had been arguing for weeks over Manuel 's girlfriend and a prior relationship she had with Clay .

She is also the mother of Manuel 's infant child , according to court records .

 Manuel and Clay , both from the Lafayette area , exchanged a volley of shots near Bourbon and Iberville streets about 1:30 a.m. Nov. 27 , authorities have said .
The melee left nine people wounded and claimed the life of Demontris Toliver , a 25-year - old tattoo artist from Baton Rouge .

Both Clay and Manuel are being held without bail on one count of first - degree murder and nine counts of attempted murder .
