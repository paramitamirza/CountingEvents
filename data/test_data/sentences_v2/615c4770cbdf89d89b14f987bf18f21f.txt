Deputies say 16-year - old shot 11-year - old brother
Snohomish County Sheriff officials report that the boy was possibly shot accidentally by an older sibling .

By Deborah Horne and KIRO
7 Staff
 A 16-year - old Snohomish County boy appeared in court Monday .
Deputies say he shot his 11-year - old brother .

Kyle Alcorn and his neighbors at the Barisa Apartments near Everett were on edge just after noon Sunday .

" Concerning , " said Alcorn , " Because there 's kids here . "

A swarm of Snohomish County Sheriff 's deputies descended on their neighborhood on the hunt for a shooter .

" They were searching for them , " said Alcorn .
" They 've been out for miles and they were still searching . "

But while deputies and their K-9 units were looking for the suspect , they got word they were on the wrong track .

" They determined that in fact , no one had fled , " said Snohomish County Sheriff 's Lt .
David Bowman . " That the young boy had been shot by an older brother . "

And that the older brother , 16 , was still inside the apartment in Building K.
 " The sibling is being talked to by deputies and detectives , " said Lt .
Bowman .

The victim was transported to Harborview Medical Center in critical condition .
At least one parent accompanied him to Seattle .
Monday morning , he was in serious condition .

Deputies spent all afternoon trying to figure out what led to the shooting .

Then just before 6 p.m. Sunday came word that the older brother was under arrest ; that the community has nothing to fear .

" We know who was involved , " said Lt .
Bowman . "
And in this case there 's no further danger to the public . "

Deputies have not given a motive .

The teenaged brother is being held at the Denny Youth Center in Everett on reckless endangerment and unlawful possession of a firearm charges .
