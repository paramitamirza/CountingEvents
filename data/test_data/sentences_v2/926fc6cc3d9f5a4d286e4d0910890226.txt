Teen surrenders after girl injured in Germantown accidental shooting
Action News has learned a 17-year - old has surrendered to police after another teen was critically injured while playing with guns in Germantown .
It happened after 3 a.m. Wednesday at home on Bloyd Street near East Woodlawn Street .
Officers arrived to find the 14-year - old victim with gunshot wounds to the hand and chest .
She was rushed to an area hospital in critical but stable condition .
Investigators soon determined a group of teens at the home were playing with the weapon when one of the teens fired it once , hitting the girl .
Firefighters were called to help police search for the weapon .
Action News is told they found a rifle and a handgun on the roof of the home .
Police believe the person who fired the weapon is an acquaintance of the victim and say the shooting appears to have been accidental .
A 17-year - old surrendered to police Wednesday night , but has not been charged .
It is the second accidental shooting involving teenagers in Germantown in as many weeks .
Exactly one week ago , on June 29th , 13-year - old Chris Mackey died after a gun found by a group of teenagers at a home on East Walnut Street went off .
A 15-year - old and 17-year - old have been charged in connection with that case .
