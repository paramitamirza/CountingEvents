Arrests made 10 months after man shot , killed during dice game at TSU
NASHVILLE , Tenn. ( WKRN )
– Metro police said Tuesday afternoon two men were arrested in Memphis in the shooting that killed a 19-year - old on Tennessee State University ’s campus last fall . NEWLINE
Police said Cameron Selmon was playing dice on October 22 , 2015 when a fight broke out in an outside area near the president ’s office . NEWLINE
At least two men fired shots , killing Selmon and injuring three other nearby TSU students . NEWLINE
U.S. Marshals arrested Christopher Gatewood , 20 , at his mother ’s Memphis home on Friday .
He remains jailed in Shelby County .
News 2 learned Tuesday night Gatewood was a student at the university at the time of the shooting but has not been enrolled since fall semester 2015 . NEWLINE
Robert Tunstall , also 20 , was arrested on Tuesday after he visited his parole officer on an unrelated charge . NEWLINE
Both men have been indicted by the Davidson County Grand Jury on one count of first - degree murder , three counts of attempted second - degree murder , and three counts of aggravated assault , among other charges . NEWLINE
Police said Gatewood and Tunstall are believed to have fled to Memphis shortly after the shooting took place .
They will be returned to Nashville in the near future and booked into the Metro jail . NEWLINE
TSU released a statement Tuesday afternoon thanking the Metro Police Department for their work in the case . NEWLINE
“
We commend Metro - Nashville Police , especially the lead detective , and others for their commitment to finding and arresting the individuals responsible for this horrific crime .
Our thoughts and prayers remain with the families as the judicial system will now take over the process of prosecuting these individuals .
The university will continue to assist Metro police with its on - going investigation related to this case .
” NEWLINE
Both men have previous criminal histories NEWLINE Gatewood was arrested in Nashville four days before the fatal shooting and was charged with possession/casual exchange of a controlled substance .
He pleaded guilty to the charges in March and was sentenced to 10 days in jail . NEWLINE
