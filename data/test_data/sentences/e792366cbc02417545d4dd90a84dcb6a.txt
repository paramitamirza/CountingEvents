4 wounded in Downtown shooting following Fourth of July fireworks

Pittsburgh police said an argument led to a shooting Monday night Downtown that wounded four people shortly after the end of the city 's Fourth of July fireworks display . NEWLINE
Authorities are looking for the shooter , a young man police said was dressed in black , and a second young man who was arguing with the shooter .
Police said the second man was thought to be wearing a white dress shirt and a white undershirt . NEWLINE
The most seriously injured was a woman who was shot in the head .
She is in critical but stable condition , police said . NEWLINE
A man and a boy were wounded in the leg and are both listed in stable condition . NEWLINE
All three patients were taken to UPMC Mercy in Uptown , said Public Safety Department spokeswoman Sonya Toler . NEWLINE
The fourth shooting victim is a 15-year - old girl who was shot in the leg .
She was taken to Children 's Hospital of Pittsburgh in Lawrenceville , police said . NEWLINE
The incident happened about 10:30 p.m. near the corner of Seventh and Liberty avenues , near the Wood Street T station , police said . NEWLINE
The Fourth of July fireworks began about 9:30 p.m. and concluded about 10:05 p.m. Thousands of spectators gathered in Downtown , particularly in Market Square , to catch a glimpse of the display . NEWLINE
Yellow police tape and squad cars blocked sidewalks and closed streets to traffic near the scene of the shooting . NEWLINE
Several police officers responded earlier in the night to Market Square where a large crowd of teenagers and young adults had gathered . NEWLINE
Officers worked to keep the crowd under control .
They drove a police car through the square with sirens blaring to disperse the crowd at about 8:40 p.m.
