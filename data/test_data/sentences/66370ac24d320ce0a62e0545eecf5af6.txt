SPPD : Suspect in party bus drop - off shooting arrested Saturday
By Peggy Kelly NEWLINE Santa Paula News NEWLINE Santa Paula Police made an arrest Saturday after an early morning shooting left five men wounded…and the man taken into custody was one of those injured in the melee that occurred during a party bus drop - off . NEWLINE
One victim of the shooting that took place at about 2:47 a.m. July 18 is in critical but stable condition . NEWLINE
Eric Medrano , 22 , of Santa Paula , was arrested for suspicion of attempted murder Saturday at approximately 10 p.m. NEWLINE
According to SPPD Sgt .
Cody Madison , “ Medrano was one of the initial reported ’victims’ however through investigation he was determined to be a suspect , ” and after he was medically cleared at the hospital he was transported to the SPPD Station for processing . NEWLINE
Medrano , who was on parole , was later transported to Ventura County Main Jail in Ventura ; although bail was initially set at $ 500,000 an increase was requested that raised it to $ 1 million . NEWLINE
Since Medrano was also on active parole at the time of his arrest a no bail hold was also placed on him . NEWLINE
Police are looking for other suspects in the incident that occurred near the corner of Ojai - Santa Paula Street ( Highway 150 ) and High Street when a party bus reportedly returning from Los Angeles dropped off a large group of up to 50 people . NEWLINE
Many , according to a witness , were intoxicated . NEWLINE
People started fighting — an altercation that SPPD Commander Ish Cordero said could have started on the bus — and witnesses said they heard 15 to 20 gunshots from what they believed was more than one shooter .
When the shooting stopped the suspects took off in a vehicle . NEWLINE
All the men that were wounded are 20 to 35 years old . NEWLINE
Santa Paula Fire EMTs and ambulances also responded to the scene where people were screaming and crying including those according to a witness that were “ hysterical…and getting in the way of the firemen and ambulance guys , ” trying to treat the wounded . NEWLINE
Cordero said officers had to “ restore order ” at the scene that he described as “
chaotic… ”
