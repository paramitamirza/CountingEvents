13 shot Sunday morning at Bridgeport house party NEWLINE BRIDGEPORT —
An apparent ambush shooting on Plymouth Street early Sunday morning that injured 13 people — including one critically — occurred during an illegal , for - profit party attended by over 100 people , police said . NEWLINE
Police Chief A.J. Perez said the party hosts , who were not identified , applied for a city permit to sell alcohol prior to the party but were turned down because such sales violate city ordinances .
He said the hosts , who rent one of two duplexes at 19 Plymouth Street , went ahead with the party anyway . NEWLINE
“
We had a tough morning , ”
Perez said during a Sunday afternoon press conference .
“
Officers encountered a chaotic scene with people suffering from gunshot wounds .
” NEWLINE
At about 1:30 a.m. Sunday morning , two shooters , hiding behind six foot hedges at a next door property at 31 - 33 Plymouth Street , opened fire on the party goers with multiple weapons , including high powered handguns , police said .
No suspects have been apprehended and a motive was not yet known , police said . NEWLINE
Eleven victims sustained non - life threatening wounds to their lower extremities and one male sustained a gunshot to the face that is not life threatening One female is in critical condition at Yale New Haven Hospital with a gunshot to the face .
She is expected to survive but will require reconstructive surgery . NEWLINE
Many of the victims were taken to a hospital by private car while others were transported by ambulance , making the situation even more confusing for officers , police said . NEWLINE
Mayor Joseph Ganim called the incident a “ horrendous act that impacted so many people and , God willing , we will not have a fatality . ” NEWLINE
U.S. Sen Richard Blumenthal , D - Conn , said police will find the “ animal who committed this atrocious act of criminality , ” and called for Congress to enact “ common sense ” gun laws to take weapons off the streets of city ’s like Bridgeport . NEWLINE
