Exclusive : Woman struck by stray bullet in Passaic speaks out
Two teenagers are facing charges after an innocent bystander was struck by a stray bullet when gunfire erupted during an argument on a Passaic street Wednesday night , and now , the victim is speaking exclusively to Eyewitness News .
Police responded to a report of a shooting at Gregory Avenue and Madison Street around 8:50 p.m. , and arriving officers found 58-year - old Santa Guerrero suffering from a gunshot wound to the leg .
Guerrero told them she was getting out of her car when she heard several shots , and as she began walking toward her home , she realized she had been shot in the right thigh."They ca n't be shooting people like that , " she said in Spanish .
" They could have killed someone .
I could have been killed .
They could have killed me."Witnesses told police that two people fled the area on bicycles after the incident."I do n't know who fired the shots , " she said .
" I know there were three ( people ) , but I only saw two when the shots were fired."A short time later , police saw a teen on a bike matching the witnesses ' description .
A 16-year - old city resident was arrested near Oak Street and Myrtle Avenue .
The alleged gunman , a 17-year - old Clifton resident , was later arrested at his home."Teenagers , " she said .
" Where are their parents ?
Parents need to know where the kids are."They are charged with aggravated assault , conspiracy and weapons possession offenses and were taken to the Essex County Juvenile Detention Center .
Police say a .38-caliber revolver was also recovered and that the teens had an argument with other people prior to the shooting .
As for Guerrero , she is simply sad that she can not walk safely in her neighborhood , especially at night."This is the area , no good , " she said .
" The area no good .
No bueno."She was taken to St. Joseph 's Regional Medical Center , where she was treated and released .
