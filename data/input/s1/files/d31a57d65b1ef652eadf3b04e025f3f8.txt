Manexpected to survive after shooting himself amid standoff with Putnam sheriff ’s deputies 

Astandoff between a gunman and Putnam County sheriff ’s deputies in Bardin came to an end Friday evening when authorities said the man shot himself in the shoulder . 
The 65-year - old man , whose name has not been released , was taken to Orange Park Medical Center , but he ’s expected to survive , according to a Sheriff ’s Office spokesman .

Authorities do not anticipate the man will be charged , Capt . Hancel Woods told the Times - Union . 
The five - hour - long standoff began about 1:50 p.m. when the suicidal man , armed with a handgun and shotgun , retreated into some woods in the Bardin area and then scaled a tree , Woods said . 
Woods said the man fired about 12 rounds indiscriminately from his perch in the tree stand , but did not appear to be firing toward the deputies gathering below . 
He said the man shot himself in the right shoulder after crisis negotiators coaxed him down , but the man was conscious and alert as paramedics transported him to the hospital . 
Woods praised the “ remarkable restraint ” of the deputies involved , adding that they did a “ fantastic job . ” 
“ It ’s a pretty good outcome , ” he said . “ A lot better than it could have gone . ”
